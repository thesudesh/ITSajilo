Seventh Semester

Internet Technology

Course Title: Internet Technology

Course No.: CSC 401

Course Nature: Theory + Lab

Semester: Seventh

Year: Fourth

Full Marks: 60 + 20 + 20

Pass Marks: 24 + 8 + 8

Credit Hours: 3

Course Synopsis: To study the concept of advanced database techniques.

Course Goal: This course deals on the practical application of internetworking technologies to private intranets for information management and public internets for electronic commerce students will learn theoretical details, strategies for designing sites, techniques for creating their technical infrastructures, methods for developing content, and techniques for site deployment and management.

Contents of Chapter

Unit 1: Introduction (5 Hrs.)

History and Development of Internets and Intranets.IANA, RIR/NIR/LIR and ISPs for internet number management Internet Domain and Domain Name System,Internet Access Overview<, Optical Backbone, Marine Cables, Teleports,Satellite and Terrestrial Links

Unit 2: Internet Protocol Overview (6 Hrs.)

TCP/IP and the IP Layer overview, IPv4 and IPv6 Address Types and Formats, IPv4 and IPv6 Header Structure, Internet RFCs

Unit 3: Protocols And Client/Server Applications (6 Hrs.)

Standard protocols: SMTP, E-mail, Message (RFC22), PGP, POP, IMAP, HTTP, FTP, N-Tiered Client/Server Architecture , Universal Internet Browsing , Multiprotocol Support

Unit 4:HTTP and The Web Services (6 Hrs.)

HTTP, Web Servers and Web Access , >Universal naming with URLs , WWW Technology: HTML, DHTML, WML, XML , Tools: WYSIWYG Authoring Tools , Helper applications: CGI, PERL, JAVA SCRIPTS, PHP, ASP, .NET Applications , >Introduction to AJAX (Programming) , Browser as a rendering engine: text, HTML, gif and jpeg

Unit 5: DESIGNING INTERNET SYSTEMS AND SERVERS (8 Hrs.)

Designing of Internet System Network Architecture, Choice of platforms, Sever Concepts: WEB, Proxy, RADIUS, MAIL, Cookies, Load Balancing: Proxy Arrays,Server Setup and Configuration Guidelines, Security and System Administration Issues, Firewalls and Content Filtering

Unit 6: Internet and Intranet Systems Development (6 Hrs.)

Introductions, Benefits and drawbacks of intranets, Protocols, Structure and Scope of Networks, Intranets Resources Assessments: Network Infrastructure, Clients and Server, Resources, Intranet Implementation Guidelines , Content Design, Development, Publishing and Management , Intranet Design with Open Source Tools: DRUPAL, JUMLA , unneling Protocols: VPN

Unit 7: INTERNET AND INTRANET APPLICATIONS (6 Hrs.)

General Applications: E-mail, WWW, Gopher, Online Systems , Broadband Communications, Policy, xDSL and Cable Internet , VoIP, FoIP and IP Interconnection , Datacenters and Data warehousing, packet clearing house , Unified Messaging Systems , Fundamental of ecommerce , Concept of Grid and Cloud Computing

Laboratory Works:

Laboratory should include features like packet capturing and analysis, design of internet/intranet system, proxy administration, firewall configuration and management, VPN, implementation of IRC, Content development with JUMLA/DRUPAL mentioned in the syllabus.
